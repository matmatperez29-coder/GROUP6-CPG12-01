End of an Era: Meta to Shut Down Messenger Website in April 2026

Meta is officially pulling the plug on Messenger.com, marking the end of the platform's decade-long run as a standalone desktop experience.

Following the retirement of its standalone Windows and Mac applications in late 2025, this move marks the end of Messenger’s decade-long run as a separate desktop service. Once the site officially closes in April 2026, users attempting to access the URL will be automatically redirected to facebook.com/messages, effectively folding the chat service back into the main Facebook interface.

This consolidation poses a significant hurdle for the segment of users who utilize Messenger without an active Facebook account, as they will no longer be able to manage their chats via a web browser. While the iOS and Android apps remain unaffected, the shift is part of a broader strategy to centralize Meta’s ecosystem. By housing all desktop communications under one roof, Meta aims to streamline its global security efforts—including the deployment of enhanced scam detection and password-free passkeys—to protect its massive user base.

by Sharona Nicole Semilla • February 18, 2026 • 10:09 pm

Scientists Confirm "True" 1D Electron Behavior in Phosphorus Chains

A breakthrough at BESSY II has experimentally proven that phosphorus atoms can host genuinely one-dimensional electrons. By disentangling signals from self-assembled chains, researchers found that squeezing these atoms tighter could trigger a radical shift from semiconductor to metallic states, promising a new era of tunable quantum electronics.
